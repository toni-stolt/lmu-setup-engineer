User Guide

Getting the best
from the AI advisor.

Learn how to write effective problem descriptions and understand what the AI analyses — so you get expert-level setup advice, not generic suggestions.

How It Works
1
Upload Telemetry
Drop in your MoTeC .ld file from any LMU session. Select the lap you want to analyse.
2
Describe the Issue
Tell the AI what the car is doing wrong — corner, phase, speed, and what it feels like.
3
Get Advice
The AI cross-references your telemetry data with LMU setup theory and delivers specific, prioritised recommendations.
Writing Good Descriptions
Good — specific and actionable
"I have snap oversteer at Arnage — slow right-hander. It happens at throttle application around 70–80 km/h. The rear breaks away suddenly, no warning. It's worse on the inside kerb. I'm running a fairly stiff rear ARB."
Bad — too vague
"The car feels loose in corners."
Good — phase and speed context
"Understeer at the apex of high-speed corners — Porsche Curves and Indianapolis. The front pushes wide in the middle of the corner. No issue at entry or exit, just mid-corner."
Bad — missing critical detail
"Understeer. Fix it please."

Tips by Issue Category
↗
Understeer

The AI needs to know when and where the understeer occurs to distinguish corner phases.

  • Corner entry push: mention trail-braking behaviour, how hard you're turning in
  • Mid-corner push: mention the steady-state speed and whether it's high or low speed
  • Exit push: mention throttle application timing and corner exit speed
  • Describe specific corners by name — high-speed corners need different fixes to low-speed hairpins
  • Note if it's worse in one type of corner than another
↩
Oversteer & Snap

Snap oversteer is one of the most dangerous handling issues. The AI needs as much context as possible.

  • Entry snap: is it under braking while turning? Mention if trail-braking is involved
  • Mid-corner: does the rear move at steady throttle? Note the cornering speed
  • Exit: is it at initial throttle pickup or further through the power application?
  • Does it happen on kerbs specifically? Kerb-induced snap has different causes
  • Does the car give warning or is it sudden? Sudden = different setup cause
⚡
Traction & Exits

Traction issues are often addressed first with TC settings before touching the mechanical setup.

  • Mention your current TC settings (TC, TC Slip, TC Cut) — these are adjusted first
  • Is it wheelspin, or is the car also moving laterally? (snap vs. pure wheelspin)
  • What corner type? Slow hairpins vs. medium exits have different solutions
  • Mention the car speed when it starts — 60 km/h hairpin exit vs. 120 km/h mid-speed
  • Does it happen on cold tyres only, or throughout the stint?
⊡
Braking & Rotation

Braking diagnosis is very different between GT3 (has ABS) and prototypes (no ABS). Mention your car class clearly.

  • For lockups (non-GT3): which end/wheel is locking? Front or rear?
  • For GT3: mention your ABS setting — ABS 9 (Understeer) is the competitive default
  • Is there instability under braking, or pure lockup? Instability can be suspension-related
  • Does it happen in straight-line braking, or while turning in (trail-braking)?
  • Mention the specific braking zones if you know them (e.g. Ford Chicane at Le Mans)
〜
Bumps & Kerbs

Bump and kerb issues are primarily addressed with fast dampers, not slow dampers.

  • Which kerbs specifically? Sausage kerbs, flat kerbs, or rumble strips?
  • What does the car do over the bump — bounce, snap, or just lose grip?
  • Does it affect both ends equally, or is one axle worse?
  • Is the issue the initial hit or the aftermath? (landing vs. recovery)
  • Are you running packers that might be too stiff? High-speed bumps + packers = trouble
